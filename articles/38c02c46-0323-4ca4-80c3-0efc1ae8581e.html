While multimodal survival prediction models are increasingly more accurate, their complexity often reduces interpretability, limiting insight into how different data sources influence predictions. To address this, we introduce DIMAFx, an explainable multimodal framework for cancer survival prediction that produces disentangled, interpretable modality-specific and modality-shared representations from histopathology whole-slide images and transcriptomics data. Across multiple cancer cohorts, DIMAFx achieves state-of-the-art performance and improved representation disentanglement. Leveraging its interpretable design and SHapley Additive exPlanations, DIMAFx systematically reveals key multimodal interactions and the biological information encoded in the disentangled representations.
