核心贡献

开发了轻量级几何感知工具VANGUARD，使LLM智能体能在GPS缺失环境中恢复场景绝对度量尺度，解决VLM空间尺度幻觉问题

方法概述

利用环境中常见的小型车辆作为锚点，通过定向边界框检测和核密度估计稳健估计像素长度，结合预校准参考长度转换为地面采样距离。工具返回GSD估计值和置信度评分，支持智能体自主决策

关键结论

在DOTA v1.5基准测试中实现6.87%中位GSD误差，相比最佳VLM基线减少2.6倍类别依赖和4倍灾难性失败，证明确定性几何工具对安全自主空间推理至关重要

原文摘录

Autonomous aerial robots operating in GPS-denied or communication-degraded environments frequently lose access to camera metadata and telemetry, leaving onboard perception systems unable to recover the absolute metric scale of the scene. As LLM/VLM-based planners are increasingly adopted as high-level agents for embodied systems, their ability to reason about physical dimensions becomes safety-critical -- yet our experiments show that five state-of-the-art VLMs suffer from spatial scale hallucinations, with median area estimation errors exceeding 50%. We propose VANGUARD, a lightweight, deterministic Geometric Perception Skill designed as a callable tool that any LLM-based agent can invoke to recover Ground Sample Distance (GSD) from ubiquitous environmental anchors: small vehicles detected via oriented bounding boxes, whose modal pixel length is robustly estimated through kernel density estimation and converted to GSD using a pre-calibrated reference length.
