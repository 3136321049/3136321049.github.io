原文摘录

Mobile devices are frequent targets of eCrime threat actors through SMS spearphishing (smishing) links that leverage Domain Generation Algorithms (DGA) to rotate hostile infrastructure. Despite this, DGA research and evaluation largely emphasize malware C2 and email phishing datasets, leaving limited evidence on how well detectors generalize to smishing-driven domain tactics outside enterprise perimeters. This work addresses that gap by evaluating traditional and machine-learning DGA detectors against Gravity Falls, a new semi-synthetic dataset derived from smishing links delivered between 2022 and 2025. Gravity Falls captures a single threat actor's evolution across four technique clusters, shifting from short randomized strings to dictionary concatenation and themed combo-squatting variants used for credential theft and fee/fine fraud.
