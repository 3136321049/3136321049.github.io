原文摘录

Earth observation applications increasingly rely on data from multiple sensors, including optical, radar, elevation, and land-cover products. Relationships between these modalities are fundamental for data integration but are inherently non-injective: identical conditioning information can correspond to multiple physically plausible observations. Thus, such conditional mappings should be parametrised as data distributions. As a result, deterministic models tend to collapse toward conditional means and fail to represent the uncertainty and variability required for tasks such as data completion and cross-sensor translation. We introduce COP-GEN, a multimodal latent diffusion transformer that models the joint distribution of heterogeneous Earth Observation modalities at their native spatial resolutions.
