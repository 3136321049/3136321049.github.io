原文摘录

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems commonly adopt retrieval fusion techniques such as multi-query retrieval and reciprocal rank fusion (RRF) to increase document recall, under the assumption that higher recall leads to better answer quality. While these methods show consistent gains in isolated retrieval benchmarks, their effectiveness under realistic production constraints remains underexplored. In this work, we evaluate retrieval fusion in a production-style RAG pipeline operating over an enterprise knowledge base, with fixed retrieval depth, re-ranking budgets, and latency constraints. Across multiple fusion configurations, we find that retrieval fusion does increase raw recall, but these gains are largely neutralized after re-ranking and truncation. In our setting, fusion variants fail to outperform single-query baselines on KB-level Top-$k$ accuracy, with Hit@10 decreasing from $0.51$ to $0.48$ in several configurations.
