原文摘录

The extraction of critical information from crime-related documents is a crucial task for law enforcement agencies. Named-Entity Recognition (NER) can perform this task in extracting information about the crime, the criminal, or law enforcement agencies involved. However, there is a considerable lack of adequately annotated data on general real-world crime scenarios. To address this issue, we present CrimeNER, a case-study of Crime-related zero- and Few-Shot NER, and a general Crime-related Named-Entity Recognition database (CrimeNERdb) consisting of more than 1.5k annotated documents for the NER task extracted from public reports on terrorist attacks and the U.S. Department of Justice's press notes. We define 5 types of coarse crime entity and a total of 22 types of fine-grained entity.
